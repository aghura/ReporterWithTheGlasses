A Thought

That if I from a lower age of man
Would look upon I from a higher one
And powder would mask his masquerade
Would I recognize him by his talks and his thoughts?
I fear not
For as the powerful play went on
Amidst the verse I forgot who I was

Perhaps the crowd calls it growth
And growth it may be.
Why are you sad dear bluebird?
It’s just a scene, let it be.
I don’t want growth, he says, this growth is not for me
For once I was a bubbly carefree bluebird
And now this painful maturity makes it difficult to breathe
