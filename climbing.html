Briefly elaborate on an activity, organization, work experience, or hobby that has been particularly meaningful to you.

I feel like Mowgli. The wind rushes through my hair, sending chills down my spine. I look down at the tiny people who’ve fixed their gaze upon me. As I climb higher, I struggle to rest my feet on the rock shaped holds. I reach a point wherein the only possibility to go higher, I figure, is by lifting my left leg above my right hand and doing some sort of impossible daredevil stunt. Restricted by my human body, I climb lower, trying to find a different path. The tiny people shout instructions and I follow. My hand can’t reach the handle. I try jumping and eventually fall down by a height of about two stories. I enjoy dangling by the pulley system and land myself like a superhero in movies!

Be it writing codes for computer programs or struggling in the middle of a narrative, climbing has taught me the significance of sometimes undoing my work and getting different people’s perspectives. In the end, it’s not always going to work, but that doesn’t mean you can’t enjoy failure and keep trying again till you succeed!
