Why I Write.

In my eighth-grade school textbook, Shakespeare defined the world as a stage and “all the men and women merely players.” He continued to describe the different stages of man starting from infancy to death, chronicling a purpose for each. I find his words and comparisons similar to those presented in free-verse poet Walt Whitman’s “O Me! O Life!” wherein the celebrated American writer once again visualizes life as a theatrical performance and motivates all actors to contribute their own unique “verse.” To write is to present my verse to the world.

In his poem “Bluebird,” Charles Bukowski describes a delicate being living in his heart that personifies his deepest emotions, feelings, and thoughts. She’s a secret he keeps from everyone, to whom he solitarily talks at night, but when she cries to be let out, he braves her struggle and mutes her voice. Over time, I have come to realize many more writers have talked about their bluebird in literature, each calling her by a different name. Audre Lorde boldly talks of her bluebird as “that one little piece inside you that wants to be spoken out, and if you keep ignoring it, it gets madder and madder and hotter and hotter, and if you don’t speak it out one day it will just up and punch you in the mouth from the inside.” Buzz Lightyear iconically describes it as his “inner voice” and activates her by pressing a red button on his fashionable spacesuit. To write is to speak my bluebird and express my emotions to the world.

Help

A friend once wrote, “if the girl from 11 a.m. would look upon the girl from 11 p.m., she would disagree with her.” If I look back upon an Aryaman from eighth grade, I question his thoughts and his beliefs. Over the vestiges of time, I seem to have forgotten him. To write is to remember who I was.

Who Am I?

Imagine a black and white film starring a puny imaginative kid who believes himself to resemble Keanu Reeves with his long black wavy hair. Despite people’s continuous efforts to disprove his good looks (wink-wink), he embodies an annoyingly attractive perseverance to his beliefs, much to the likes of Woody in Toy Story. Under the fabulous mane of ecstasy, lies his mind, a bubbling cauldron of dead rats, cannibalistic ideas, and apple-flavored skittles (disgusting yet satisfying). When he’s not fantasizing about the taste of human blood, one may find him at his desk, pondering in thought behind those round Lennon glasses as he chews the end of an ill-fated pen. In other words, I’m weirdly obsessed with cheddar cheese, red VW beetles, New York City, Apple products, classic music from the 1900s, and appetizing Japanese pens. And it gives me immense pleasure to welcome you to my blog, to feed you bubbling colorful skittles from this belly of the cauldron that lives in an otherwise dark monochromatic world.
