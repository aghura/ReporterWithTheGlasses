Share a time when you had a conversation with a person or a group of people about a difficult topic. What insight did you gain, and how would you incorporate that knowledge into your thinking in the future?

“Let’s talk periods.” I was introduced to Madame Kiran Gandhi at the Round Square International Conference 2020 hosted by our school. Her story of running the London Marathon, bleeding free, had hooked everyone to what this bold lady had to say. Her personality was stimulating and had fostered an hour-long discussion on the unhygienic conditions of women in rural as well as urban India in our Barazza group. Unsanitary university washrooms, expensive sanitary pads unaffordable to 64% of Indian women, long-standing taboos, and poor menstrual hygiene are just a few problems that stain the lives of women. Of course, the biology class had taught what menstruation is, but due to the lack of health education and awareness among people, most were unaware of this alarming situation. These discussions fostered dialogue on solutions and unraveled so many problems. Be it conversations on fourth-wave feminism, the global menstrual stigmas, or the general problems faced by school-going girls in their day to day lives, it helped me and so many others depose that discomfort of speaking on difficult issues that are rather left ignored and avoided in discussions. I realize that change cannot occur when such difficult issues are avoided. Awareness as well as discussions are imperative for society to tackle these problems and gain a deeper understanding of the extent of the matter.
