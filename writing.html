Reflect on a belief or opinion held by you, which you questioned and examined. What initiated your questioning? Describe the intellectual process you undertook to examine this belief or opinion.Explain the results of your reflection and the changes you saw in your belief or opinion.

“Once upon a time, there was a crocodile.” Often, as a second-grader, I’d be seen chewing the end of my pencil and scribbling stories on a pack of sticky notes, but in the end, I was never wholly satisfied with the stories I wrote. When taught the idea of “brainstorming, drafting, editing, and revising” in my school in New York, I was initially reluctant to transform my swift writing style into this long structured process, boasting of a “superior” ability in writing. However, participation among my peers, sharing ideas and progressing narratives sentence by sentence, helped me learn different styles and exposed me to other authors’ unique perspectives. As I brainstormed various elements such as the description of the five senses and drafted narratives with the aim to “show” and not “tell,” I sensed my stories improving. Even today, I’m seen chewing the end of my pencil and scribbling stories on a pack of sticky notes, but in the end, I truly enjoy the stories I write.
